Passport and Visa

Any foreigners that intents to enter the Bangladesh for the conference must required a valid passport and a Bangladeshis visa issued by your local or nearest Bangladeshis embassy or consulate.

Nationals of visa waiver or visa-free countries may visit Bangladesh for up to 90 days without a visa. However, due to the ongoing pandemic, Bangladesh has temporarily suspended its visa waiver agreement with some countries. If you are unsure whether you need to apply for a visa, please visit the Bangladesh Visa Portal where you can search for your visa requirements by country/region, purpose of entry, etc.

Starting from September 2023, travelers from visa-waiver countries are required to get Bangladesh Electronic Travel Authorization (K-ETA) online prior to their departure to Bangladesh. For detailed information about K-ETA, please go to the official site.

The Bangladesh has currently suspended the visa on arrival and offers only the option of obtaining a visa through a government diplomatic office, either an embassy or a consulate, or through designated travel agencies depending where you will be applying for your visa. This process of applying for a visa involves having to attend an appointment in person and supplying an embassy with a range of supporting documentation depending on the requested visa type. If you require a visa, you are strongly advised to start your visa application early enough to be sure to have your visa before you depart. The visa application process may take longer in certain countries, so please consult your local or nearest Bangladeshis embassy or consulate.
